  1. Whether the Respondent's failure to deliver the 3D character pack within the 14-day SLA constitutes a material breach of the smart contract escrow agreement.
  2. If material breach is established, whether the Claimant is entitled to a full refund plus liquidated delay damages.
  3. Whether the Respondent's partial-delivery offer (40% completion) qualifies as performance sufficient to bar the claim.

R · Governing Norms

1. Forum & Choice of Law

  • Forum agreement — by the model clause embedded in the disputed smart contract (Trianum Rules Annex A), the parties have agreed to resolve this dispute under the Trianum Hybrid Arbitration Rules, sole jurisdiction of the Trianum General Court.
  • Substantive law — by party agreement under the model clause, the substantive law of the merits is the law of the Republic of Korea.
  • Seat — Seoul, Republic of Korea.

2. Applicable Substantive Law

  • Korean Civil Code Art. 544 — right of rescission upon delay in performance.
  • Korean Civil Code Art. 390 — damages for non-performance.
  • Korean Civil Code Art. 398 — liquidated damages and contractual penalty.
  • Smart Contract §2.3 — the 14-day delivery SLA clause.

3. Procedural Rules (referenced)

  • Trianum Rules Article 13 — admissibility of evidence (no submissions accepted after the close of the evidence period).
  • Trianum Rules Article 23 & 26 — single-instance procedure (arbitrator signature after the 7-day Reconsideration Window elapses).

A · Analysis

The Tribunal evaluates the eight items of evidence submitted by the parties via IPFS and XRPL Memo during the evidence period (April 1 – April 15, 2026):

  • E1 [IPFS bafy…x7q] Respondent admitted, four hours before SLA expiry, in chat: "tomorrow is unrealistic" → establishes intentional delay.
  • E2 [Block 89,442,108 / 2026-04-15 09:00 KST] On-chain timestamp at SLA expiry → fixes objective time of breach.
  • E3 [XRPL Memo 0x4d2…8f1c] Absence of any delivery-confirmation transaction during the 14-day window → direct evidence of non-performance.
  • E4 [Settlement offer 2026-04-12] Respondent's 40% partial-delivery proposal → falls short of the relief sought; not substantial performance.
  • E5 [ETH Global 2025 Standards] Industry-standard 14-day SLA for digital 3D-asset delivery → confirms reasonableness of the SLA.
  • E6 [Korean Supreme Court, 2023 Da 12345] Precedent recognizing full rescission for breach of digital-asset delivery → direct doctrinal basis.
  • E7 [ICC Award No. 2024/567] Comparable international award granting full refund plus liquidated damages in an analogous NFT-delivery dispute → comparative basis for remedies.

E1, E2, and E3 establish the objective facts of material breach; E4 disqualifies Respondent's defense; E5 confirms the reasonableness of the SLA; E6 and E7 supply the governing doctrine and comparative remedy basis; E8 supports the quantum.
